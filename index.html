Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our welding services and processes

We have extensive experience working with a wide range of aluminum alloys, including the most common grades such as 5083, 6061, 6063, 7075, and 2024. Each alloy has unique characteristics and welding requirements. Our certified welders are trained in the specific techniques needed for each alloy to ensure optimal weld quality and mechanical properties. We can handle both standard applications and specialized alloys for aerospace, marine, and industrial applications. We recommend consulting with our team about your specific alloy requirements to ensure we can deliver the best results for your project.

Quality assurance is central to everything we do. We provide comprehensive testing including visual inspection, penetrant testing (PT), radiographic testing (RT), and ultrasonic testing (UT) as required by your specifications. All our welders maintain current AWS D1.2 certifications for structural aluminum welding. We also hold ISO 9001 certification, demonstrating our commitment to quality management systems. Documentation and test reports are provided with every project, and we can accommodate specific customer requirements or industry standards such as ASME, DNV, or ABS standards. Your project's quality requirements guide our testing protocols.
